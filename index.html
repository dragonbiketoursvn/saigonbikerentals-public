Licenses

Enforcement of traffic laws in Vietnam is far stricter now than in the past. The days of just paying a small 'fine' to get out of trouble are gone. If you ride without a valid license and cause an accident you may face significant civil and even criminal liability.

Fortunately, the process of obtaining a Vietnamese license is straightforward for anyone possessing a valid temporary residence card (TRC) AND WE COVER THE COST OF OBTAINING A VIETNAMESE LICENSE IF YOU RENT A BIKE FROM US!.

(If you already possess a valid motorcycle driving license from an ASEAN member state or a valid motorcycle driving license and International Driving Permit from another country which is party to the 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Traffic then you may legally operate any class of bike covered by your current license and do not need to obtain a Vietnamese license unless you wish to ride bikes with a greater engine capacity than allowed by your foreign license.)

WHAT BIKES AM I PERMITTED TO RIDE FOR EACH LICENSE CATEGORY?
NO LICENSE With no license you may legally ride any gas-powered motorbike with an engine capacity of less than 50cc (or electric bicycles with max speed of less than 25km/h and less than 250W max power output).
A1 With an A1 license (or equivalent) you may legally ride any motorbike with an engine capacity of up to 125cc or with a maximum power output of up to 11kW (for electric bikes).
A With an A license (or equivalent) you may legally ride any motorbike.

Ok, so how do I get one?

The process is straightforward but it will require you to invest some time. No matter how much you may pay to an agent you will have to make at least one visit to the license office in District 3 (if doing a license conversion), and, if taking a test, to one of the hospitals which performs the required healh exams and to the testing center in Tan Phu district.

The exact steps involved depend on which type of license you want to get and what type of license you currently hold, if any. Please use the dropdowns below to find out which steps apply in your case.

Insurance

What you need to know!

You should obtain travel insurance prior to your arrival in Vietnam. As a general rule, your insurance will not cover any claims resulting from a motorbike accident if you are found to be in violation of Vietnamese traffic laws. This means that in addition to not speeding, running red lights, or driving under the influence of alcohol (ANY alcohol), you must possess either a valid Vietnamese motorbike license or an International Driving Permit together with a foreign license that permits you to operate a two-wheeled vehicle with an engine capacity of at least 110cc. Your International Driving Permit must be obtained in the same country in which your license was issued.

Vietnam is a signatory to the Vienna Convention on Road Traffic and recognition of IDPs is explicitly codified in Vietnamese law. However, as the law only covers IDPs issued by countries which are party to the Convention, we strongly advise you to contact your embassy to determine whether you can legally use an IDP in Vietnam. The text of the law (in Vietnamese) can be seen here.The relevant section is Clause 11, which states:

"1. Persons in possession of an International Driving Permit issued by a country which is signatory to the Vienna Convention must carry their IDP and a foreign license appropriate to the class of vehicle being driven when operating a motor vehicle and must obey the traffic laws of Vietnam."

2. In the case that a person's IDP is temporarily revoked for violation of Vietnamese traffic laws, the time period of the revocation may not exceed the time period which this person is permitted to stay in Vietnam."
